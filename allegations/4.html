Key Defence Arguments

Iterative Scheduling: Construction schedules are inherently iterative. They depend on regulatory approvals, weather conditions, material availability, and client decisions. Providing a rigid schedule at contract signing is neither standard practice nor practical for residential construction.
Detailed Stage Plan Provided: On 13 Oct 2025, Krithika provided a comprehensive stage-by-stage plan covering the sequence of activities before wall construction. This demonstrates planning and transparency.
Formal Document Delivered: A formal construction ledger and schedule document were shared on 31 Dec 2025, providing the client with a structured project timeline as requested.
Client's Own Delays: The client's decision-making timelines and family consultations also affected the overall schedule. Construction project timelines are a collaborative responsibility between the contractor and the client.

Risk Assessment

Low risk. The contract explicitly provides an 8-month construction period plus 2-month grace (Section F). A formal timeline document (CONSTRUCTION_Ledger.pdf and Sheets Document.pdf) was shared on 31 Dec. Stage-by-stage plans were communicated via chat on 13 Oct. Construction schedules are inherently iterative and depend on approvals, weather, and client decisions — all of which caused delays in this project.
