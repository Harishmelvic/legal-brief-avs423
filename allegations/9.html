Defence & Counter-Evidence

Contractor's Defence

1. Contract Itself Is the Protection

The signed construction agreement between both parties serves as the primary legal protection. Both parties are bound by its terms and conditions, including provisions for dispute resolution, scope of work, and payment milestones. The contract is enforceable in law.

2. Stage-Wise Payment Structure

The payment structure is milestone-based, which inherently protects the client. Payments are tied to completion of specific construction stages, ensuring that the contractor must complete defined work before receiving the corresponding payment. This structure prevents overpayment relative to work completed.

3. Work Was Never Abandoned

Despite multiple disagreements and difficult exchanges between the parties, construction work was never abandoned. All construction stages proceeded continuously, demonstrating the contractor's commitment to project completion.

4. Client Validated the Payment Structure

The client himself acknowledged the fairness of the payment arrangement:

"There's absolutely no problem in paying the stage wise money."
Contract & Ledger Evidence

Contract Section H: 5-Stage Milestone Payment Schedule

5. Detailed Milestone Payment Structure

The signed contract (Section H) establishes a precise 5-stage payment schedule tied to construction milestones:

Stage % Amount (Rs.) Milestone Trigger Status
Stage 1 6% 3,00,000 Design package initiation Fully Paid
Stage 2 39% 18,50,000 Foundation to Ground Floor PCC Fully Paid
Stage 3 40% 19,00,000 GF PCC to Terrace Headroom 13L Paid / 6L Outstanding
Stage 4 14% 6,50,000 Finishes (painting, plumbing, electrical) Pending
Stage 5 1% 52,000 Retention (6 months post-handover) Pending

This milestone-based structure means the client only pays as work is completed and verified. It IS the payment protection mechanism.

6. 10-Year Structural Warranty (Contract Section F)

The contract provides a 10-year structural warranty from the date of handover, plus 1-year general maintenance warranty. This is the strongest form of post-completion protection available in residential construction — the contractor remains legally liable for structural defects for a full decade.

7. Stage 5 Retention: Rs. 52,000 Held for 6 Months

Contract Section H specifies that Rs. 52,000 (1% of contract value) is retained by the client for 6 months after handover. This retention serves as a financial safeguard against defects discovered post-completion — a standard industry protection mechanism built into the contract.

8. Construction Ledger: Full Payment Transparency

The CONSTRUCTION_Ledger.pdf shared on 31 December 2025 documents every payment received with full transparency:

Stage 1: Rs. 3,00,000 (fully paid, online)
Stage 2: Rs. 18,50,000 (fully paid — Rs. 2L + Rs. 2L + Rs. 3L + Rs. 10L cash + Rs. 1.5L online)
Stage 3: Rs. 13,00,000 paid (Rs. 10L cash + Rs. 3L online), Rs. 6,00,000 outstanding

Total documented payments: Rs. 34,50,000. This transparent ledger IS the payment protection the client claims is missing.

Risk Assessment

Low Risk

The signed contract itself is the client's protection — it binds both parties legally. The 5-stage milestone payment schedule (Contract Section H) ensures work precedes payment at every phase. The 10-year structural warranty (Section F) provides the strongest post-completion protection available in residential construction. The Stage 5 retention of Rs. 52,000 held for 6 months post-handover adds a further financial safeguard. The CONSTRUCTION_Ledger.pdf (shared 31 Dec 2025) documents Rs. 34,50,000 in payments with full transparency — this IS the payment protection the client claims is missing. Work was never abandoned despite disputes. The client himself validated the payment structure: 'There's absolutely no problem in paying the stage wise money.'
