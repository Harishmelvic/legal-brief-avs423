Unauthorized Additional Charges

High Severity Moderate Defence

"Where and how the additional charges are appearing all of a sudden without my prior approval? I don't remember discussing or agreeing to increase the size of the main sump."
11 November 2025 — Chat Line 619
Client (Mr Perinbanathan)
"Your team just randomly comes up with additional payment requests all of a sudden which is taking a toll on the trust."
Defence & Counter-Evidence

Contractor's Defence

1. APCO Blocks — Client's Own Choice

The client himself chose the premium APCO blocks option. The additional cost of Rs. 64,350 was clearly communicated before placing the order.

Chat Line 591
Client (Mr Perinbanathan)
"I would prefer to go only with Apco due to superior compressive strength."

Reference: Cost communicated at Chat Line 596

2. Contract Clause on Additional Work

The contract clearly defines the scope. Any work outside the agreed scope constitutes additional work and is chargeable. Harish communicated this principle clearly:

Chat Line 635
Harish (Contractor)
"Any thing apart from the contract is considered as additional work."
Chat Line 687
Harish (Contractor)
"The cost in the contract is calculated based on what is specified in the same. Any additional work will be carried out with additional cost."

3. Septic Tank — Scope Change Explained

The septic tank was a necessary scope change. Harish explained the technical reason and asked the client for a decision before proceeding.

Chat Line 663
Harish (Contractor)
"It is converted to septic and soakpit for that capacity."

Reference: Client asked for decision at Chat Line 668

4. Minutes of Meeting (MOM) Confirms Mutual Agreement

The client's own Minutes of Meeting dated 1 December 2025 acknowledges the process for handling additional charges:

1 December 2025 — Chat Line 730
Client's MOM
"Calculation and basis for additional works carried out by contractor will be shared/clarified by contractor. The mutually agreed charges to be cleared later by owner."

5. Contract Clause G.4 Expressly Covers This

The signed contract (Page 10, Section G - Liabilities, Clause 4) explicitly states:

Contract Page 10, Clause G.4
Signed Contract
"Any deviation in the project plan as per the wishes of the client shall involve additional costs which shall be borne entirely by the client."

The client signed this clause on all 12 pages. He was fully aware that client-driven changes would incur additional costs.

6. Block Cost in Contract vs APCO Upgrade

The contract specifies block costs at Rs. 68/block (8") and Rs. 42/block (4"). The APCO blocks cost Rs. 88 and Rs. 53 respectively — a clear, quantifiable premium of Rs. 20 and Rs. 11 per block that the client chose voluntarily.

Risk Assessment

Moderate Risk

Some additional charges were client-driven (APCO blocks), while others arose from scope changes (septic tank conversion). The contract clause G.4 explicitly covers additional costs for client-driven deviations. The contractor should prepare a detailed breakdown of each additional charge with full justification, the date the change was communicated, and evidence of the client's approval or acknowledgement for each item.
