Defence & Counter-Evidence

Contractor's Defence

1. Festival Holidays — Industry-Standard Downtime

Labour shortages during festival seasons are standard across the Indian construction industry. Krithika explained the situation transparently:

Chat Line 497
Krithika (Team)
"Due to festivals there is be labour shortage from pooja the workers dint come to site yesterday... the workers will again taking leave during Diwali (it is considered in the timeline)."

2. Weather Conditions

Wet soil conditions prevent certain construction activities for safety and structural integrity reasons. Ajay documented the conditions:

Chat Lines 361–362
Ajay (Site Team)
"The soil seems to be wet. And the workers were on leave for 3 days."

3. Industry-Wide Labour Shortage

Labour shortages during festival seasons (Pooja, Diwali) are a well-documented, industry-wide phenomenon across the Indian construction sector. This is not specific to this contractor or project. Any construction project in this region would face similar disruptions during these periods.

4. Grace Period in Contract

The contract includes an 8-month construction period with grace provisions for unforeseen circumstances. Festival holidays and weather disruptions fall within the category of foreseeable seasonal delays that the grace period is designed to accommodate.

5. Work Resumed After Every Stoppage

After every period of stoppage, work was restarted and progress updates were shared with the client. No stoppage was permanent or indicative of project abandonment. The contractor maintained ongoing engagement with the project throughout.

Risk Assessment

Moderate Risk

Multiple work stoppages are documented in the chat record. However, they were caused by festival holidays, adverse weather conditions, and labour market conditions — not contractor negligence or abandonment. The contractor should demonstrate that these delays were factored into the project timeline and that the cumulative impact did not exceed the contractual grace period. A timeline mapping each stoppage to its cause (festival, weather, etc.) would strengthen this defence considerably.
