Allegation 15

MCA & RoC Legal Threat

Severity: High Defence: Strong

Defence Story

On 26 March 2026, after three months of silence in the WhatsApp group, the client's mother sent a message threatening to file complaints with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) and Registrar of Companies (RoC). The notification was sent to three email addresses belonging to the contractor. However, MCA and RoC deal with corporate governance under the Companies Act — matters like director misconduct, fraud, or statutory non-compliance. They have no jurisdiction over construction contract disputes between a company and an individual client. The appropriate legal forum, as specified in Contract Section I, is arbitration in Tirunelveli, Tamilnadu — the exclusive dispute resolution mechanism both parties agreed to when signing the contract. Furthermore, Contract Section G.7's penalty clause applies only to contractor-caused delays, while Section F explicitly exempts delays caused by client payment defaults. The threat came not from the client or a lawyer, but from the client's mother, and it was described as a 'notification' and a 'gesture' — not a formal complaint. After a 3-month silence, this appears to be a pressure tactic rather than a legitimate legal remedy. The contractor should respond through the contractually agreed arbitration forum.

Defence Points

MCA/RoC jurisdiction: MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) and RoC (Registrar of Companies) deal with corporate governance, not construction contract disputes. This appears to be a misdirected complaint. These bodies regulate company registration, director compliance, annual filings, and corporate fraud — none of which are relevant to a construction services dispute.
Intimidation tactic: The threat came after a 3-month silence (last message was 8 January 2026). This appears to be a pressure tactic rather than a genuine legal remedy. The sudden escalation without prior correspondence suggests an attempt to coerce rather than resolve.
No prior legal notice: No formal legal notice under Section 80 CPC was served. A WhatsApp message is not a substitute for proper legal procedure. Any genuine legal action would require a formal notice served through recognised legal channels.
Client's mother sent it: The notification was sent by the client's mother, not the client himself or a legal representative. This weakens its formal standing. A complaint to a regulatory body should originate from the aggrieved party or their authorised legal counsel.
Notification, not complaint: The client himself calls it a "notification" and a "gesture" — not a formal complaint. The language used ("giving you a reasonable time to react") frames it as a warning, not a filed action. It appears to be a last-resort pressure tactic with no procedural backing.

Contract Dispute Resolution Provisions

Contract Section G.7 — Penalty Limited to Contractor-Caused Delays: The penalty clause states penalties apply only "If the work is delayed by the contractor beyond the time". Delays caused by client payment defaults, design changes, or force majeure are explicitly excluded under Section F. The client cannot invoke penalties for delays attributable to his own non-payment of Rs. 6,00,000 on Stage 3.
Contract Section F — Payment Delays Excluded from Contractor Liability: Section F explicitly states: "The contractor will not be liable for any delay in completion of the work caused due to non-payment of dues by the client as per the scheduled timeline." The 30-day payment delay documented in the timeline sheet falls squarely within this exemption.
Contract Section I — Arbitration in Tirunelveli: The signed contract specifies arbitration in Tirunelveli, Tamilnadu as the exclusive dispute resolution mechanism. Any construction dispute should be resolved through this contractually agreed forum — not through MCA/RoC complaints, which have no jurisdiction over construction contract disputes between a company and an individual client.

Risk Assessment

Low Risk

Low risk in terms of legal merit. MCA/RoC complaints typically relate to company fraud, director misconduct, or non-compliance with the Companies Act — not construction contract disputes. The appropriate forum for a construction dispute would be a Consumer Forum or Civil Court. The contractor should consult with counsel on a pre-emptive response to ensure any MCA/RoC filing is addressed procedurally, but the substantive risk is minimal given the clear jurisdictional mismatch.
