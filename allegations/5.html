Key Defence Arguments

Substance Over Form: The purpose of weekly reports is to keep the client informed of progress. The WhatsApp group achieved this objective through real-time multimedia updates that were arguably more informative than a written weekly summary.
Medium Changed, Intent Fulfilled: While the reporting medium was WhatsApp rather than formal PDF reports, the intent — keeping the client informed with visual evidence of progress — was continuously fulfilled. 83 attachments across 10 months exceeds the volume of a weekly report.
Client Acknowledged Updates: The client thanked team members 25+ times for updates, confirming he received and valued the information being shared. This acknowledgment undermines the claim that no progress reporting occurred.
Industry Context: In residential construction in India, WhatsApp groups are the standard communication and reporting channel. Formal weekly PDF reports are uncommon in projects of this scale.
Multi-Disciplinary Team Engagement: The project architect Gladwin was added to the WhatsApp group on 10 July 2025 (Line 185) and formally introduced on Line 205. Site supervisor Ajay was also active in the group. The communication channel included the full project team — designer, architect, engineer, and supervisor — all providing real-time updates.
111 Visual Updates: Beyond the 83 file attachments in the chat, the WhatsApp export contains 71 photographs and 40 videos documenting every construction stage from ground-breaking (April 2025) through masonry and finishing work (November 2025). This visual record exceeds any standard weekly PDF report in both frequency and detail.

Risk Assessment

Low risk. 83 attachments, 244 team messages, and continuous multimedia updates across 10 out of 10 months demonstrate extensive reporting. The WhatsApp group functioned as a real-time progress channel that exceeded the utility of formal weekly reports. The client thanked the team 25+ times for updates — contradicting any claim that no reports were shared. The medium of reporting changed, but the substance was delivered in abundance.
