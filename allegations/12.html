Allegation 12

Pump Controller Left Unsecured

Severity: Low Defence: Strong

Defence Story

The borewell submersible pump was installed by the client at his own expense during his site visit on 8 August 2025. The pump controller and cables were the client's own equipment — not contractor-supplied items. The temporary shed on site had a single-phase power supply, which was insufficient for the three-phase pump controller, so the controller had to be placed in the meter box with a manual connection setup (Lines 336-338). On 14 September 2025, the client noticed the controller was left outside and warned: 'If I see once again it is left unattended in the rain without any security, unfortunately I will be forced to take it with me' (Line 452). This was a legitimate concern about client-owned equipment storage. However, the contract does not impose an obligation on the contractor to secure client-supplied equipment. No damage to the controller was reported at any point, and the client's threat to remove the equipment confirms it was his property. This is a minor site housekeeping matter, not a contractual breach.

Defence Points

Client's own equipment: The borewell pump was installed by the client at his own expense (Line 316). It is the client's property. The contractor has no custodial obligation over client-owned equipment unless expressly stated in the contract.
Operational matter: Securing client-owned equipment at a shared construction site is a site management issue, not a contractual obligation. There is no clause in the contract placing responsibility for safeguarding client-supplied items on the contractor.
No damage occurred: There is no evidence of actual damage or loss to the pump controller. The client's messages express concern about a potential risk, but no harm materialised from the alleged negligence.

Risk Assessment

Very Low Risk

This is a minor site management issue regarding client-owned equipment. No contractual obligation was breached, no damage was sustained, and the client himself acknowledged his ownership and control over the item by threatening to take it with him. This allegation has no legal merit as a contractual claim.
