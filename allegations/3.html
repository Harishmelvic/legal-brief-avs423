Key Defence Arguments

Drawings Shared Before Execution: Every structural drawing was shared before the respective construction stage began — footing layouts before excavation, column details before column casting, plinth beam drawings before plinth work.
Client Gave Technical Feedback: The client reviewed drawings and suggested adding extra bars, adjusting rod sizes, and adding plinth beams for strength. A person who never received drawings could not provide such detailed structural input.
Client Visited Site & Inspected Work: The client visited the site multiple times (28 Jun, 2 Aug, 8 Aug, 14 Sep, 6 Dec), inspected executed work against the drawings, prepared a corrections PPT, and approved work to proceed.
Client Shared His Own Reference Drawings: On 21 Jul, the client shared his Bangalore house's column and footing schedules as reference — actively participating in the structural design process rather than being kept in the dark.
Third-Party Dependency: Some structural drawings were dependent on the structural engineer, a third party outside the contractor's direct control. Krithika transparently communicated this dependency.

Risk Assessment

Low risk. The client's own messages prove he received drawings before execution, gave detailed technical feedback (suggesting extra bars, reviewing rod sizes, recommending additional plinth beams for strength), visited the site to inspect work, prepared a corrections PowerPoint, and approved construction to proceed with "Proceed with the construction work." A person who did not receive drawings could not have provided such detailed structural input. The allegation of "not shared on time" is contradicted by the client's own active engagement with every drawing received.
