Key Defence Arguments

Same-Day Response: When the client requested column and footing details on 17 Jul 2025, Harish responded the same day with the requested drawings, site video, and additional sump/septic drawings.
Third-Party Dependency: Some structural drawings were dependent on the structural engineer, a third party outside the contractor's direct control. Krithika transparently communicated this dependency to the client on 14 Nov 2025.
Drawings Were Shared: The allegation implies drawings were never shared. The chat record proves that footing layouts, column details, sump/septic drawings, and plinth beam drawings were all delivered through the WhatsApp group.

Risk Assessment

Moderate risk. The column/footing schedule was indeed delayed relative to the client's expectations. However, drawings were eventually shared, and some delays were due to the structural engineer (a third party). The contractor should document the structural engineer's timelines as supporting evidence to demonstrate that the delay was not attributable to negligence but to an external dependency chain.
