Applicable Legal Principles

Free Consent — Indian Contract Act, Section 14
Consent is said to be free when it is not caused by coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation, or mistake. The chat evidence demonstrates entirely voluntary and deliberate engagement.
Approbation and Reprobation
A party cannot benefit from a contract while simultaneously claiming it was coerced. The client invoked contract terms 5+ times in his favour.
Ratification by Conduct
Rs. 34.5 Lakhs paid voluntarily over 8+ months constitutes ratification through performance. Continued voluntary payments eliminate any claim of coercion.
Burden of Proof — Indian Contract Act, Section 16
The burden of proving coercion lies on the party alleging it. The client has provided no evidence of coercion; the chat record proves the opposite.

Evidence from the Contract Document Itself

The final executed contract (12-page e-stamped document dated 22 May 2025) provides additional irrefutable evidence against the coercion claim:

Father Signed as Witness: The client's father, M. Vasanthan, signed as a WITNESS on Page 12 of the contract. A witness to a contract attests that the signing was voluntary and observed. The witness cannot later claim the signatory was coerced — this would contradict his own attestation.
Client Signed Every Page: All 12 pages of the contract bear the client's signature ("V. Perinbanadhan"), demonstrating page-by-page acknowledgment of every clause, specification, and term. This is not a single hasty signature but deliberate, repeated consent.
"This Agreement is Irrevocable": Page 12 explicitly states: "This agreement is irrevocable." The client agreed to this term with full knowledge, after 2 months of negotiation.
E-Stamp Paper (Karnataka): The contract was executed on Rs. 100 Karnataka e-Stamp (Certificate No. IN-KA38039389272158X), purchased by Speckle Interiors Pvt Ltd. This is a formal, legally registered document — not a casually forced signature.
Contract Value Matches Client's Proposal: The contract value of Rs. 47,52,000 (1650 sqft × Rs. 2,880/sqft) exactly matches the amount the client himself proposed on 25 Mar 2025: "I have adjusted the total amount to the actual construction area of 1650 SQFT. The total amount would be about Rs. 47,52,000."

Risk Assessment

Very low risk. The chat evidence overwhelmingly disproves this allegation. The client requested the agreement, negotiated for two months, uploaded the final version himself, paid Rs. 34.5 Lakhs voluntarily, and invoked the contract 5+ times for his own benefit. The father's signature as witness, client's signature on all 12 pages, the "irrevocable" clause, and the contract value matching the client's own proposal make the coercion claim untenable. The doctrine of approbation and reprobation squarely applies.
